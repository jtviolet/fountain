Theme

This year's theme is "Inclusion and Creativity", and we are opening up the hackathon to any and all projects, security related or not. All project submissions will receive points from judging, and have a chance to win first, second, or third place prizes. For those that are looking for an edge against the competition, bonus points will be awarded to projects that are designed with the "Cloud" in mind, and that utilize FireEye products via API. To learn more about judging, see the Judging section below. Come and build something fun, cool, and innovative with your friends and coworkers!

Participation

We want to encourage as many employees as possible, regardless of technical ability, to participate in this year's hackathon to strengthen relationships, foster creativity, and learn new things. This will be a safe place to experiment with any ideas you may have, be they FireEye related or not, with free food, prizes, and other goodies to keep things engaging. While software engineering is a usually a core part of every hackathon, there are opportunities to participate this year outside of writing code.

  • Teams will need to effectively advocate their ideas to judges, so participants with video, marketing, sales, and public speaking experience can improve the chances of winning.
  • We will need volunteers to help coordinate and run the hackathon both on-site and remotely.
  • We are looking for project sponsors in every department to pitch innovative ideas for hackathon participants to work on. Also, if you have an idea but you don't know how to build it, this is a perfect way to join in!
If you are interested in volunteering, please email hackathon@fireeye.com

Judging

Projects will be judged on the following criteria:

  • LEVEL OF INNOVATION
    • Technology can be cool but not innovative—and vice versa. Apart from coolness, “level of innovation” is our take on participants’ awareness of other attempts to solve the same problem they are, and how the team positions their hackathon entry. Does it pull something off that hasn’t been pulled off before? Does it do something new and unique?
  • REALISTIC CAPABILITY
    • The two days we set aside for our teams to pause their normal course of work go by fast. Which teams built working solutions? Which bit off a little more than they could chew? What gets done in the space of a hackathon is a decent indication of the dependencies and level of effort that would be required to put the technology into production. This category is our way of quantifying that.
  • COOLNESS
    • “Coolness” is an attempt to measure the important “wow” factor: the impression the hackathon entry makes when you see it, and the gestalt of the demo. Is it a reflection of a diverse team’s ingenuity? Is it something a human being would feel delighted, excited, empowered, or even relieved to use.
All projects starts with a multiplier of 1.0x and, while we encourage any and all project ideas, this year's hackathon will award small bonus multipliers to the final score of projects that adhere to the following criteria:
  • +0.1x points for projects that have a “for the cloud” aspect – e.g. protects cloud workloads or other customer activity in the cloud
  • +0.1x points for projects that use one or more FireEye products via API (DoD, Innovation Architecture, HX/Helix API, etc.)
Judging will happen in two rounds:
  • Round one judging will incorporate Gavel, a pairwise judging algorithm developed by MIT for their hackathon. Judges will use video submissions and project files to determine the best projects based on the above criteria. Bonus points will be added after round one judging is complete. The final score will determine the top 5 projects, which will move on to round two.
  • Round two finalists will have one week (April 6th-10th) to prepare a 5 minute presentation followed by 5 minutes of questions for a panel of judges. Finalist round will take place on Monday, April 13th, with winners announced that same day. In the event of a tie, Grady Summers will decide the winner.
